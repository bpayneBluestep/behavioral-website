Admissions, referrals, and pre-admissions — one workflow, one record

Every empty bed is a revenue crisis, and every referral is a relationship you can't afford to drop.

Leads come in by phone and get noted on a sticky. Spreadsheet pipelines go cold because nobody owns the follow-up. Referral partners stop calling because nobody called them back. Families fill out the same intake packet three times — once by PDF, once by fax, and once again by phone to the admissions coordinator who can't find the first two. So we built the admissions system residential facilities actually need — referrals, leads, and pre-admissions in one record, handing off into the EHR without a handoff.

Referral network

Referral relationships live on a liaison's memory and a calendar reminder nobody set. A partner sends three patients in a quarter, then stops calling, and nobody can answer why. Primary ties and one-off referrals blur together until the next census dip — and then it's too late to rebuild.

  • Partner record for every referral source — hospital, therapist, PHP, school district — with contact history, liaison notes, and the running list of patients referred
  • Primary and secondary referral ties captured on every patient, so a single lead can credit both the referring therapist and the referring hospital without forcing a choice
  • Liaison visit tracking tied to the partner record — annual site visits and monthly calls get scheduled, logged, and surfaced before they fall off the calendar
  • Referral-source attribution on every lead, every admission, every closed-lost — so you know which partners actually convert, not just which ones call
  • Partner-performance analytics roll up from the same record — no separate report-building step, no export-to-spreadsheet reconciliation

Lead pipeline

Leads live in a spreadsheet until the spreadsheet gets out of date. A family calls back on a Thursday and nobody can answer which stage they were in, whose turn it is to follow up, or whether the clinical screen ever happened. The Friday census review asks the same question every week — was this lead ever qualified, and who owns it now — and the answer is always a dozen clicks away.

  • Every lead moves through named pipeline stages — New Lead, Qualified, Family Engaged, Admission — with stage-entry timestamps captured automatically
  • Notes stay with the lead card — every call, every voicemail, every family conversation is on the record instead of on a sticky
  • Close-won and close-lost reasons captured on the lead record for post-mortem review — insurance not covered, clinical fit, family chose another program — so the loss patterns surface instead of disappearing
  • Pipeline aging alerts flag leads that have sat in a stage too long, so the ones going cold surface before the family calls somewhere else
  • Ownership is explicit per stage — the admissions coordinator responsible for the next step is named on the lead, not implied by whoever happens to notice

Pre-admissions application

Families in crisis fill out the same intake packet three times — once on the PDF the website emailed them, once again on the fax the admissions office asked for, and a third time over the phone with the coordinator who can't find the first two. Every retype is a chance for the clinical picture to drift. Every repeat ask is a reason for a family to give up on your program.

  • Facility builds its own pre-admissions form — headers, text fields, dropdowns, and memo fields — without waiting on a vendor release
  • Families complete the form online from a phone or a laptop, save and return as authorizations arrive, and submit once instead of filing the same answers three times
  • Submitted data lands directly on the patient record — no separate data-entry step, no re-keying into the EHR at admission
  • Configurable per program, per payer, per age group — adolescent residential asks different questions than adult PHP, and the form builder reflects that
  • Every field on the patient record is the same field the family filled in — nothing retyped, nothing reformatted, nothing lost between the application and the chart

As family applications arrive, BlueIQ drafts an admission summary from the submitted data — your admissions team edits and confirms instead of writing from scratch. The summary carries into the patient's EHR record at admission.

Seamless admission into EHR

Admission day is where most systems show their seams. Pre-admissions data exported from one tool, mapped through an integration layer, imported into another, and re-validated by a clinician who now has to double-check every field the family already filled in. BlueStep's CRM and EHR are one unified patient record — no API to configure, no migration step to run, no integration layer to maintain.

  • Pre-admissions data flows into the same record as the EHR chart — the family application and the clinical chart are two views of one patient record, not two databases stitched together
  • Admission promotes the lead to a patient on that same record — no export step, no import step, no reconciliation between systems
  • No API configuration, no third-party connector, no data-migration tool — admission is a status change on one record, not a handoff between two
  • Clinical team picks up the chart with everything the family submitted already populated — demographics, insurance, presenting concerns, medical history — no retyping at admission
  • Every downstream module (treatment plan, eMAR, documentation, billing) draws from the same patient record the admissions team populated — one database, start to finish

Analytics

The monthly admissions review asks the same four questions every time — how many leads, what converted, which partners delivered, why did we lose the ones we lost — and the answers live in five different spreadsheets that haven't agreed with each other since February. Reconstructing those numbers manually on a Friday evening is nobody's idea of strategic work.

  • Lead volume and trend across 30, 60, and 90-day windows — the shape of the funnel, visible at a glance, without pulling a report
  • Conversion rates by pipeline stage and by referral source — the rate that leads reach admission, broken out so the weak stages surface
  • Close/loss reason rollups — the top reasons leads drop out of the pipeline, so the patterns worth fixing surface before the next census review
  • Referrals by partner, with performance comparison across the referral network — which partners are sending volume, which relationships are cooling
  • Dashboard surfaces weekly without manual report-building — the numbers your admissions director would assemble by hand are already assembled
