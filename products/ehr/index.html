EHR

Clinical documentation, treatment plans, and eMAR, built for behavioral health.

Built for behavioral health, not retrofitted

Fifteen years documenting what actually happens inside residential treatment.

Notes get written twice: once for the record, once for the payer. Utilization reviews get assembled from scratch every week. Surveys arrive and the documentation has to be there, exactly, the first time. So we built the record system residential clinicians actually need, shaped over fifteen years inside a working facility.

Clinical documentation

Residential clinicians document the same clinical picture three times a week: session note, weekly summary, utilization review. BlueStep captures the picture once and reuses it everywhere it's needed.

  • Individual, Family, and Group session notes with fields that match how the session actually runs
  • Medical and nursing notes in the same record as clinical notes. No parallel chart.
  • DAP or SOAP format, chosen per note type and switchable per payer contract

BlueIQ reads the clinical record and drafts the utilization review narrative. Your team edits and submits. It lives inside the EHR, not in a separate tool your clinicians have to learn.

Individual session notes

One-on-one sessions in a DAP or SOAP template sized for a 50-minute residential hour.

Family session notes

Participants, session focus, and family-system observations, kept with the patient chart.

Group session notes

Shared group content plus a patient-specific impression. No duplicate typing across the roster.

Psychiatrist notes

Evaluation, medication review, and mental status, linked to active orders and the treatment plan.

Medical and nursing notes

Assessments, shift notes, and incident documentation in the same record. One chart, not two.

Treatment plans

Ninety-day reviews arrive whether or not the plan still fits, and outpatient templates force clinicians to rebuild the structure every cycle. BlueStep's builder holds the structure so your team can focus on the clinical picture.

  • Problem > Goal > Objective > Intervention, every level traceable to a diagnosis
  • A built-in residential library of problems, goals, and interventions, plus custom templates your program owns and edits itself
  • Ninety-day review cycle built in: prior plan carries forward, prompts show what needs an update

Problem

The diagnosed condition, pulled from the diagnostic workup, not retyped. Problems tie to DSM/ICD diagnoses so the picture stays consistent from intake through discharge.

Goal

The measurable outcome you and the patient are working toward. Written once, reviewed every ninety days, revised when the picture changes.

Objective

The concrete, time-bound behavior that shows progress. Target dates, evidence fields, and status, so a reviewer sees where the patient actually is.

Intervention

The specific clinical action, linked to the responsible clinician and the required frequency, so nothing falls through between shifts.

eMAR

Paper MARs get lost between shifts. A missed dose doesn't surface until the next med pass, and the reason ends up on a sticky note. The eMAR generates the daily schedule from active orders and keeps the record tight through every shift change.

  • Daily schedule generated from active orders. No retyping, no transcription errors.
  • Tap, time-stamp, initial. PRN reasons and missed-dose exceptions captured at the point of care and routed to the nurse supervisor.
  • Shift-change handoff view: what was given, what was missed, what's due

Lock, unlock, and cosignature

Cosignature requirements vary by licensure, state, and payer contract, and surveyors want every unlock and override time-stamped and visible. BlueStep captures all of it automatically.

  • Notes lock on submission. The record freezes the moment the clinician signs.
  • Admin unlock with captured reason and full audit trail. Corrections are visible, not hidden.
  • Licensure-aware routing: the system knows a provisional-licensed therapist needs an LCSW cosign and routes accordingly

Lock on submit, unlock on demand

Notes lock after submission. Admins unlock for correction with a captured reason. The record shows what changed, who changed it, and why.

Admin override with audit trail

Every unlock and override is time-stamped, reason-required, and visible in the record's history. Surveyors see the full chain without you assembling it.

Dynamic multi-cosignature

Chains are driven by licensure and program rules, not hard-coded. The note routes to whoever the current rule requires.

Form and feature-level permissions

Role buckets break down inside residential. A nurse documents med passes but never edits therapy notes. An admissions coordinator reads the record but writes nothing to it. Picking a role from a list can't capture that, so BlueStep doesn't try. Permissions are granted per user, per form, per feature.

  • Per-user permissions, not role buckets that force compromises
  • Four access levels per scope: reader, author, editor, no access
  • Per-form and per-feature granularity: editor on clinical notes, reader on billing, no access to unlock controls
This is not role-based. You don't pick 'Nurse' or 'Therapist' from a list. You grant each person reader, author, editor, or no access on each form and each feature. Real residential teams don't fit role buckets.
